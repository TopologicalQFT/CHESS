# Move Selection Checklist

Run this for EVERY move, in order. Steps 1–2 are where games are won and lost.

## 1. Forcing moves first — theirs, then yours
Examine ALL checks, captures, and direct threats, even "obviously bad" ones.
- **Their last move:** what does it newly attack? What did it STOP defending? (A piece that moved away may have abandoned something.)
- **Hanging pieces:** list every piece of yours under attack; count attackers vs defenders. Then do the same for their pieces.
- Scan [[Tactical Patterns]] — both directions.

## 2. Considering a capture or trade?
- Calculate the FULL exchange sequence to the end. Who's ahead when the dust settles?
- Trade pieces when ahead in material; avoid trades when behind ([[Plans by Position Type]]).
- Trade queens when up material or under attack; keep queens when attacking.

## 3. No tactic? Improve your worst piece
- Which of your pieces does the least? Find it a better square.
- Rooks → open/half-open files; double them when possible.
- Knights → outposts (squares no enemy pawn can ever hit), especially central ones.
- Bad bishop? Put pawns on the opposite color or trade it off.
- No idea what the position wants? Read [[Position Evaluation]].

## 4. Sanity check before committing
After your intended move: what is the opponent's BEST reply? Does it win material or mate? Verify the move is in your legal moves list. See [[LLM Blunder Modes]] for the mistakes to specifically rule out.
