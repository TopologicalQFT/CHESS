# Position Evaluation

Who is better, and why? Material first, then position.

## Material
Q=9, R=5, B=3, N=3, P=1. Two minor pieces ≈ rook + pawn (usually better). Bishop pair is worth ~½ pawn in open positions.

## Positional factors (roughly in order of weight)
1. **King safety** — open lines toward a king outweigh a pawn or two. An attack on the king trumps grabbing material.
2. **Piece activity** — an active rook beats a passive one by about a pawn's worth. Count how many squares each piece controls.
3. **Pawn structure** — doubled/isolated/backward pawns are long-term targets. Passed pawns are gold ([[Endgame Golden Rules]]).
4. **Space & center** — control of e4/d4/e5/d5; more space = easier maneuvering.

## Using the evaluation
The evaluation tells you the PLAN, not the move:
- Better king safety → attack the king
- More active pieces → open the position (pawn breaks, trades that help you)
- Better structure → trade pieces toward an endgame
- Their weak squares/pawns → pile up on them

Then pick concrete candidate moves with the [[Move Selection Checklist]] and match the plan to [[Plans by Position Type]].
