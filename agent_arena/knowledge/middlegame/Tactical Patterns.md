# Tactical Patterns

Scan for these EVERY move — as opportunities for you and threats against you.

| Pattern | Recognize it by |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Fork** | One piece attacking two — knight forks (c7/f7/e6 classics), pawn forks, queen double-attacks |
| **Pin** | A piece that can't move without exposing something bigger behind it. Pile attackers onto pinned pieces |
| **Skewer** | Big piece in front of a small one on a line — a check or attack drives it away, winning what's behind |
| **Discovered attack** | Moving one piece unmasks another's attack. Deadliest with check (the moving piece can grab anything) |
| **Back-rank mate** | King stuck behind its own pawns, no escape. Keep a luft (h3/h6) or guard the rank; exploit theirs |
| **Remove the defender** | One piece holds their position together — capture it, chase it, or overload it |
| **Overloaded piece** | A defender with two jobs can't do both — give it a third or cash in one |

**Combination hint:** tactics usually start from a forcing move (check/capture/threat). If you sense something but can't see it, list every check and capture again — slower.

Pinned-piece warning: a pinned piece does NOT really defend anything and may not legally move at all — recheck pins before trusting a defender count ([[LLM Blunder Modes]] #1).
