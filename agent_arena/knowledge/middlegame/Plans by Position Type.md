# Plans by Position Type

What to DO when there's no tactic. Identify your position type, follow its plan.

## You're ahead in material
Trade **pieces** (not pawns), simplify into a won endgame, stay safe. Decline complications — boring wins. See [[Endgames Index]] when it thins out.

## You're behind in material
Avoid trades, keep queens on, create complications and threats. Endgames lose themselves — middlegame chaos is your only chance.

## Closed center (locked pawn chains)
Maneuver slowly. Prepare a pawn break (f4/f5, c4/c5, b4/b5) where you have more space. Attack on the wing your pawn chain points toward. Example: [[French Defense]] structures.

## Open center (no central pawns)
Piece activity is everything. Rooks to open files NOW, knights to outposts, target loose pawns. Tempo matters — don't waste moves.

## Opposite-side castling
It's a race. Push pawns at THEIR king, every tempo counts, don't defend passively. The first one to open a file usually wins.

## Same-side castling, quiet position
Improve your worst piece ([[Move Selection Checklist]] step 3), grab space, create a second weakness in their camp before cashing in the first.
