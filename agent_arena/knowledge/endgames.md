# Endgame Technique

Read when ≤ 12 pieces remain or queens come off. Endgames reward precise, principled play — exactly where checklists beat intuition.

## Golden rules
1. **Activate your king** — it's a fighting piece now; march it to the center.
2. **Passed pawns must be pushed** — and escorted by the king or rook from behind.
3. **Rooks behind passed pawns** — yours or the opponent's.
4. The defender wants to trade pawns; the attacker wants to trade pieces.

## Basic checkmates (know cold, don't stalemate!)

### K+Q vs K — the box
Shrink the king's box with queen moves a knight's-distance away from their king (never adjacent until mate). When their king is on the edge, walk your king up and mate. **Stalemate alarm:** queen on the 6th rank vs king on the 8th — always leave them a move until your king arrives.

### K+R vs K — the fence
Rook cuts the king off on a rank/file (the fence). Walk your king up in opposition; when kings face each other, check with the rook to push them back a rank. Repeat to the edge, mate on the edge.

### K+2 minor pieces: B+B mates, B+N mates (hard, drive to the bishop's-color corner), N+N cannot force mate.

## King & pawn endings

### The square rule
Can the defending king catch a running pawn? Draw the square from the pawn to the promotion rank — if the king can step into it, the pawn is caught.

### Opposition
Kings facing each other with one square between: whoever does NOT have to move wins the standoff. Use it to escort your pawn: **king in front of the pawn, pawn behind** wins (except rook pawns).
- King on the 6th rank in front of its pawn = always winning.
- Defender's drawing method: take the opposition, stay in front.

### Rook pawns (a/h files) are drawish
If the defending king reaches the corner, it's a draw — even up a pawn. Avoid trading into rook-pawn endings when winning; aim for them when losing.

## Rook endgames (the most common)
- **Active rook over material** — a rook stuck defending a pawn is worth less than a marauding one. Give a pawn for activity if needed.
- **Lucena (winning):** pawn on the 7th, king in front, build the "bridge" with the rook on the 4th rank.
- **Philidor (drawing):** defender's rook on the 3rd rank stops the king's advance; when the pawn pushes, check from behind forever.
- Cut the enemy king off by a file from the action.

## Practical endgame checklist (every move)
1. Can any pawn promote by force? Count the race in moves — exactly.
2. Is my king as active as possible? Is there a stalemate risk (mine or theirs)?
3. Before trading the last pieces: is the resulting K+P ending actually won/drawn? (Square rule + opposition — calculate, don't hope.)
4. Passed pawn candidates: which pawn majority can create one? Start it early.
