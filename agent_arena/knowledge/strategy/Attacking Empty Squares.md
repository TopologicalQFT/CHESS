# Attacking Empty Squares

Beginners attack pieces. Strong players attack SQUARES — an empty square can be worth more than a pawn.

## Why empty squares matter
A piece can be defended or moved away; a weak square is forever. Target squares that:
- Cannot be defended by enemy pawns ever again (pawn already passed it or captured away) — an **outpost**
- Sit next to the enemy king — a future mating net hook
- Serve as the only entry point into their position (7th rank, open-file invasion square)

## How to use them
1. **Identify** the squares in THEIR camp no pawn can ever control (look at their pawn structure, not their pieces).
2. **Reroute** a knight (best) or rook toward that square — maneuvers like Nf3–d2–c4–e5 are normal, not slow.
3. **Fight for it first** if they can still contest it with pieces — trade off the defenders of the square before occupying.

## Square-attacking in the attack
A mating attack is a fight for the squares around the king, not for material:
- Sacrifices that strip pawns (Bxh7+, Rxg7) are buying the SQUARES h7/g7, not the pawns.
- Before sacrificing, count which of your pieces can reach the squares around their king vs which of theirs defend those squares. Attackers must outnumber defenders.

## Defensive mirror
Your own weak squares (holes in front of a castled king: f3/h3 or f6/h6 after a pawn move) invite the same treatment — avoid pawn moves that create permanent holes near your king without a concrete reason.
