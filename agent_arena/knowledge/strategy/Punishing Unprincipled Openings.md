# Punishing Unprincipled Openings

The opponent broke the [[Opening Principles]]. There IS a fine to collect — but only if you punish correctly. Wrong punishment loses games faster than the original sin.

## The golden rule of punishment
**Punish with development, not with greed.** The refutation of bad opening play is almost always "develop faster and open the center", almost never "win a pawn".

## By violation

### Early queen (2. Qh5 types)
Develop pieces WITH TEMPO by attacking the queen: ...Nc6, ...Nf6, ...g6 — each developing move gains time because the queen must keep moving. After 4-5 free developing moves you're effectively a full opening ahead. Don't chase the queen with pawn moves that weaken your position; chase with pieces you wanted to develop anyway. ⚠ First verify their crude threat (Qxf7#?) is actually stopped — see [[Opening Traps]].

### Excessive wing pawns (a4/h4/g4 pushes)
Strike in the CENTER immediately (e5/d5 breaks). Wing play with an unstable center fails — that's the principle they violated. Open central files while their king is stuck; their wing pawns become targets later.

### Neglected development / pawn grabbing
If they spent 3+ moves winning a pawn while you developed: open lines NOW even at the cost of that pawn or another (gambit logic). Count tempi: each extra developed piece ≈ 1/3 pawn, and in open positions more. Attack before they catch up — slow play refunds their fine.

### Premature attacks (2-piece "attacks")
Defend precisely with developing moves, decline panic weaknesses (...g6/...h6 only if forced). An attack without development collapses on its own; the attacker's pieces end up offside and you hit the center.

## The discipline
After winning the opening battle, you have an ADVANTAGE, not a win. Switch back to normal play ([[Move Selection Checklist]], then [[Converting an Advantage]]) — overpressing a +1 position into a -2 position is a classic [[LLM Blunder Modes]] pattern (#3: pursuing your plan past their threats).
