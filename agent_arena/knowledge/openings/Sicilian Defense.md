# Sicilian Defense (facing it as White)

After 1. e4 c5 — keep it simple with the Open Sicilian:

```
1. e4 c5 2. Nf3 d6 3. d4 cxd4 4. Nxd4 Nf6 5. Nc3
```

**The plan:** Be2, O-O, then either f4 (attack) or Be3+Qd2 (English Attack setup). 

**Key points:**
- Watch the **d5 square** — it's the strategic heart of the Sicilian. A knight landing on d5 permanently is usually winning.
- Don't grab pawns with the queen (especially b7) — Sicilian players live off that bait.
- Black's counterplay comes down the half-open c-file: don't leave c2 undefended behind.

If Black plays 2...Nc6 or 2...e6 instead of 2...d6, the same recipe works: d4, Nxd4, Nc3, develop, castle.
