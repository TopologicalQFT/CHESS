# Opening Traps

Known ambushes. Check this note whenever an early opponent move looks "free", weird, or too aggressive.

## Traps aimed at YOU

### Scholar's Mate (you are Black)
2. Qh5 or 3. Qf3 with Bc4, eyeing f7. **Defense:** ...Nc6 first (guards e5), then ...g6 hitting the queen, then ...Nf6. Never play ...g6 before e5 is defended (2...g6?? 3. Qxe5+ wins the rook).

### Fried Liver (you are Black, Italian move order)
After 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4, the move 3...Nf6?! invites 4. Ng5! hitting f7 with serious threats. **Avoidance:** play 3...Bc5 instead (see [[Italian Game]]).

### Légal's Mate pattern (both colors)
A queen that looks free (often after a knight "blunders" Nxe5) — check for Bxf7+ followed by Nd5/Ng5 mating nets BEFORE taking. If a queen is free, ask why.

### Noah's Ark (you are White, Spanish)
Your a4-bishop gets buried by ...b5, ...c4. Mind the b3 escape square timing.

## Traps working FOR you

### Elephant Trap (you are Black, QGD)
After 1. d4 d5 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bg5 Nbd7 — if White grabs 5. cxd5 exd5 6. Nxd5?? then 6...Nxd5! 7. Bxd8 Bb4+! 8. Qd2 Bxd2+ 9. Kxd2 Kxd8 wins a piece.

### Qd4 fork vs early Bxc6+Nxe5 (you are Black, Spanish)
4. Bxc6 dxc6 5. Nxe5? Qd4! forks knight and e4-pawn — material back with interest.

## The meta-rule
Free material in the first 10 moves is guilty until proven innocent. Calculate the capture sequence fully ([[Move Selection Checklist]] step 2) before grabbing.
