# K+R vs K — the Fence Method

1. **Build the fence:** the rook cuts their king off along a rank (or file) — the king can never cross it.
2. **March your king up** behind the fence toward their king.
3. **Take the opposition:** when the kings face each other directly (one square between, their move or yours arranged right), **check with the rook** — their king must retreat a rank. The fence advances one rank.
4. **Repeat** until their king is on the edge. Mate: rook checks along the edge, your king directly opposite theirs guarding all escape squares.

## Details that matter
- If their king attacks your rook, slide the rook far along the SAME rank (the fence holds; rook range is free).
- If their king won't cooperate (steps aside instead of facing yours), make a **waiting move** with the rook along the fence — zugzwang forces them back toward opposition.
- Stalemate risk is low but real in the final position — confirm they have a legal move before any non-checking move near the edge.

Takes ~15-20 moves from the worst case. Watch the 50-move counter only if pawns are long gone.
