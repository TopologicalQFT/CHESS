# Rook Endgames

The most common real ending. Two named positions decide most of them — know both.

## Activity above all
- An active rook is worth ~a pawn over a passive one. **Never tie your rook to defending a pawn** if you can activate it instead — give the pawn back for activity if needed.
- Rooks behind passed pawns ([[Endgame Golden Rules]] #3).
- Cut the enemy king off by a file from the action.

## Lucena — the winning method (you have K+R+P vs K+R)
Your pawn on the 7th, your king in front of it (on the promotion square), their king cut off:
1. Rook to the **4th rank** (your 4th).
2. King steps out of the pawn's way toward the rook's side.
3. They check from behind; walk the king toward the rook's "bridge": the rook blocks the check on the 4th rank. Pawn promotes.

## Philidor — the drawing method (you DEFEND K+R vs K+R+P)
1. Park your rook on your **3rd rank** — their king cannot advance past it without the pawn's help.
2. The moment the pawn steps onto that rank, swing your rook to the 8th and **check from behind forever** — their king has no shelter.
3. Keep your king in front of the pawn the whole time.

## Quick rules
- Rook endings a pawn down are usually drawn with active play — never resign them early.
- Connected passed pawns on the 6th beat a rook.
