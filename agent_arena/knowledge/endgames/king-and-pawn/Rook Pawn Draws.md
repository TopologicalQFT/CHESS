# Rook Pawn Draws

The a- and h-pawns are the drawish exception to everything in [[Opposition]].

## The rule
If the defending king reaches the **corner** (or even just the promotion square's area: for an h-pawn, g8/h8/g7/h7), it is a DRAW — even with attacker's king and pawn nearby. There is no way to evict a king from the corner without stalemate.

Also drawn: attacker's own king trapped in FRONT of its rook pawn on the 8th — the defender just shuffles, and the attacker is stalemated-in or repeats.

## Practical consequences
- **Winning a pawn race by one tempo?** Check WHICH pawn — winning with a rook pawn while their king heads to the corner = draw, not win.
- **You're losing?** Steer into a rook-pawn ending: trade everything else, sprint the king to the corner.
- **You're winning?** Keep a non-rook pawn alive at all costs; avoid trades that leave only a/h pawns.
- Wrong-colored bishop + rook pawn (bishop doesn't control the promotion corner) is also a famous draw — same corner logic.
