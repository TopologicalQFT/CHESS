# Opposition

Kings facing each other with ONE square between them: whoever does **NOT** have to move "has the opposition" — the other king must give way.

## Why it decides K+P vs K
**Winning setup (attacker):** king IN FRONT of its pawn, pawn behind. Take the opposition, force their king aside, escort the pawn through.
- Your king on the **6th rank** in front of the pawn = ALWAYS winning, opposition or not.
- Pawn leading the king ("pawn in front") = usually only a draw.

**Drawing setup (defender):** stay directly in front of the pawn; when forced back, retreat STRAIGHT back (not diagonally) and grab the opposition each time the kings face off. Never get pushed out of the pawn's path.

## Using it as the attacker
1. Move your KING, not the pawn, until their king is forced away from the path.
2. Pawn moves are precious "tempo" moves — spend one exactly when you need to flip whose move it is (regain the opposition).
3. The pawn promotes when your king controls the promotion square.

## Distant opposition
Kings on the same line with an ODD number of squares between (3 or 5): same logic — step toward each other and the near-opposition converts.

⚠ Exception: none of this saves a rook pawn — see [[Rook Pawn Draws]].
