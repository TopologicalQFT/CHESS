# K+Q vs K — the Box Method

1. **Shrink the box:** place your queen a knight's-move away from their king (NOT adjacent, NOT giving check). Each time their king moves, mirror it with the queen, keeping the knight's-distance — the king's available "box" shrinks every move.
2. **Drive to the edge:** repeat until their king is confined to one edge file/rank.
3. **STOP the queen, bring the king:** once they're stuck shuttling on the edge, leave the queen alone and walk your king up to within two squares.
4. **Mate:** queen mates on the edge square, protected by your king (or from the side along the edge).

## ⚠ Stalemate alarms
- Queen a knight's-distance from a CORNERED king (king in the actual corner) — that exact pattern can stalemate. Give them one extra square until your king arrives.
- Never check pointlessly — every check lets their king pick its square. Checks come only at mate.

Counting: this takes at most ~10 moves from anywhere. If you've made 15+ queen moves without progress, you're shuffling — re-read step 1.
