# Opening Repertoire

Read this during moves 1–10. Follow the repertoire while the opponent stays in known lines; when they deviate, fall back to the principles in CLAUDE.md (develop, castle, center).

## As White: play 1. e4

### Against 1...e5 → Italian Game
```
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. c3 Nf6 5. d3 d6 6. O-O
```
**Plan:** quiet buildup; c3+d4 break later, Re1, Nbd2-f1-g3. Don't rush.
- If 3...Nf6 (Two Knights): play 4. d3 (safe) — avoid 4. Ng5 complications.
- If 2...Nf6 (Petrov): 3. Nxe5 d6 4. Nf3 Nxe4 5. d4 — equal but easy to play.

### Against 1...c5 (Sicilian) → keep it simple
```
1. e4 c5 2. Nf3 d6 3. d4 cxd4 4. Nxd4 Nf6 5. Nc3
```
**Plan:** Be2, O-O, f4 or Be3+Qd2. Watch the d5 square. Don't grab pawns with the queen.

### Against 1...e6 (French)
```
1. e4 e6 2. d4 d5 3. e5 c5 4. c3 Nc6 5. Nf3
```
**Plan:** hold the d4–e5 chain (c3, Be2), space advantage on the kingside. Your bad piece is the c1-bishop, theirs is the c8-bishop.

### Against 1...c6 (Caro-Kann)
```
1. e4 c6 2. d4 d5 3. exd5 cxd5 4. Bd3 Nc6 5. c3
```
**Plan:** Exchange variation — simple symmetrical structure, develop and play for small edges.

## As Black

### Against 1. e4 → play 1...e5, aim for the Italian/Spanish setups
```
1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 (Italian) — mirror White's plan
3. Bb5 a6 4. Ba4 Nf6 5. O-O Be7 (Spanish, Morphy Defense) — solid; play d6, O-O, b5 later
```
- Against 2. Qh5?! : play 2...Nc6 (defends e5), then 3...g6 hitting the queen. Never 2...g6?? 3. Qxe5+.

### Against 1. d4 → Queen's Gambit Declined
```
1. d4 d5 2. c4 e6 3. Nc3 Nf6 4. Bg5 Be7 5. e3 O-O
```
**Plan:** solid triangle (e6+d5), develop, then ...c5 or ...dxc4+...b5 break. Don't hold cxd4 pawns greedily.

### Against 1. c4 (English) or 1. Nf3
Play ...e5 or ...d5 + ...Nf6, develop normally toward the same structures.

## Traps to AVOID falling into

| Trap | The bait | The punishment |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Scholar's Mate | 2. Qh5 / 3. Bc4 aiming at f7 | Defend with ...Nc6 + ...g6; never ignore Qxf7# |
| Fried Liver | as Black: 3...Nf6 4. Ng5! | Avoid by playing 3...Bc5 instead of 3...Nf6 |
| Légal's Mate | "free" queen via Nxe5 sac | If a queen looks free, look for Bxf7+/Nd5 follow-ups |
| Elephant Trap (QGD) | 5. Nxd5?? grabbing a pawn | ...Nxd5 6. Bxd8 Bb4+! wins the queen back +piece |
| Noah's Ark | Spanish: White's Ba4 trapped | As White, mind ...b5+...c4 trapping your bishop |

## Universal opening rules (when out of book)
1. Knights before bishops; develop toward the center.
2. Castle by move 8 unless there's a concrete reason not to.
3. Don't move the queen before minor pieces are out.
4. Don't take poisoned pawns (b2/b7, g2/g7) with the queen.
5. Meet a flank attack with a center break.
