# Converting an Advantage

Winning a won position is a separate skill. Most blown games die here, not in the opening.

## The conversion rules
1. **Kill counterplay first, cash in second.** Before grabbing more material or attacking, ask [[Prophylaxis]]'s question: what is their only hope? Remove it. A position with zero counterplay wins itself.
2. **Trade pieces, not pawns** when material is up ([[Plans by Position Type]]). Each piece trade brings the win closer; each pawn trade brings the draw closer.
3. **Don't open the position near your king** — the side with less material wants chaos. Deny it, even at the cost of speed.
4. **Two weaknesses principle:** if one target isn't enough to break through, fix it (tie their pieces to it), then create a SECOND weakness on the other wing. Defenses that cover two fronts collapse.
5. **No hurry.** Improve every piece to its best square before forcing matters; the advantage doesn't run away. (Exception: advantage IN TIME/initiative — that one rots; spend it fast.)

## The endgame gate
Before simplifying into an endgame, verify it's actually winning — [[Endgames Index]] checklist, especially [[Rook Pawn Draws]] and [[Opposition]]. "Up a pawn" + wrong ending = draw.

## Mental traps while winning
- Relaxing the [[Move Selection Checklist]] — their checks/captures/threats still come FIRST every move
- Playing "good enough" moves fast — that's how +5 becomes +1 becomes 0
- Spite-checking and pointless captures — every move must still have a job
