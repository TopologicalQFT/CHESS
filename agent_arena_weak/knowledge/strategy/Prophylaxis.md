# Prophylaxis

The strongest question in chess: **"What does my opponent WANT to do?"** — asked before every move, answered concretely.

## The method
1. Pretend it's their move. Find their best plan or move (not just threats — plans: which break, which maneuver, which trade do they dream of?).
2. Ask: does my intended move allow it, ignore it, or prevent it?
3. If you can prevent their idea while improving your position (a move that does BOTH), that's usually the best move on the board.

## Classic prophylactic moves
- A "mysterious" rook move to the file they want to open — pre-occupying it
- h3/h6 before back-rank issues or before ...Bg4/...Ng4 ideas become annoying
- Kb1/Kh1 sidestep before opening lines (removes future checks/pins on the king's diagonal/file)
- Trading their ONE active piece before it organizes an attack
- a4/a5 freezing their pawn-minority before it rolls

## When prophylaxis beats action
- You're better but there's no breakthrough yet → strangle counterplay first ([[Converting an Advantage]])
- Closed positions where plans are slow — killing their only plan leaves them planless
- They have exactly one source of counterplay (one break, one open file) — spend a move to seal it

## The balance
Prophylaxis is not passivity. If checking their plan reveals it's harmless or too slow, IGNORE it and proceed with your own ([[Move Selection Checklist]] step 1 already filters real threats). Preventing phantom threats is how advantages evaporate.
