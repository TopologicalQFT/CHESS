# Weaknesses — what the word actually means

A weakness is a defect that is **(1) permanent enough** that it can't easily be repaired, **(2) attackable** — the enemy can actually bring force against it, and **(3) costly to defend** — it ties pieces to babysitting duty. All three. Miss one and it's a feature, not a weakness.

The deep part is (3): **a weakness is a tax on coordination, paid elsewhere.** The isolated pawn rarely falls — instead two pieces guard it all game, and the position is lost on the other wing where you're effectively down two pieces.

## The catalog, with recognition signals

### Pawn weaknesses (static — exploit slowly)
| Weakness | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Isolated pawn | No friendly pawns on adjacent files — no pawn can EVER defend it |
| Backward pawn | Neighbors advanced past it; can't advance safely; on their half-open file |
| Doubled pawns | Two on one file (grave when also isolated) |
| Hanging pawns | A duo with no neighbors on half-open files — strong advancing, weak once fixed |
| Over-extended pawn | Pushed beyond piece support — can be fixed and harvested |
| Pawn islands | Count the groups; more islands = more edges needing piece-defense |

### Square weaknesses (the most permanent)
| Weakness | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Hole | A square no friendly pawn can ever attack again — fatal when central and knight-occupiable ([[Attacking Empty Squares]]) |
| Blockade square | The square in front of an isolated/backward/passed pawn — rent-free parking |
| Color complex | Pawns fixed on ONE color + the other color's bishop traded → a NETWORK of weak squares |
| Lost fianchetto bishop | g7/g2 bishop traded while the king lives there — the worst color complex |

### King weaknesses (SCALING — re-evaluate after every major trade)
| Weakness | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| King in center | Past move ~10 with the center opening — DYNAMIC and urgent; evaporates when they castle |
| Hook | Any pawn moved in front of the castled king (h6/g6/f3...) — the lever for storms and sacs |
| Open lines to the king zone | Count enemy pieces aimed or aimable in 1–2 moves at the king's sector |
| Back rank | No luft + their heavies can reach the rank |

### Piece & functional weaknesses (DYNAMIC — exploit NOW or lose them)
| Weakness | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Overloaded defender | Role map: one piece, 2+ defensive jobs |
| Loose pieces | Zero defenders (hanging scan) — every tactic's raw material |
| Bad bishop | Own FIXED central pawns on its color — a tall pawn |
| Out-of-play piece | Can't reach the decisive side within 2–3 moves — you're down a piece THERE |
| Trapped piece | ≤2 escape squares near the edge (mode-13 audit before claiming it) |
| Development lag | Count developed pieces both sides; a big lead + opening files = their whole position is one dynamic weakness |

### Line weaknesses
| Weakness | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Open/half-open file into your camp | They have rooks for it, you can't contest it — the ENTRY squares are the real weakness |
| Open lines to your Q/K | The [[Finding Tactics]] alignment signals, read defensively |

## The two meta-rules (apply to EVERY entry you write down)

1. **The attackability test:** name the enemy piece that will actually attack it — or it's cosmetics, not a weakness. A backward pawn on a closed file goes on NO list.
2. **The tempo tag:** mark each weakness **static** (exploit by slow maneuvering; FIX it first so it can't advance or trade itself away) or **dynamic** (forcing moves now — it evaporates in two moves). Misjudging the tag is fatal in both directions: rushing a static weakness wastes it; maneuvering against a dynamic one lets it castle away.

## The standing strategic query

**Do they have TWO weaknesses on opposite wings yet?** One weakness is almost always defensible — the defense concentrates. If they only have one, *creating the second one is the plan* ([[Converting an Advantage]]). The attacker switches between two fronts faster than the defense can re-deploy.

Run the catalog both directions: your own weaknesses, honestly listed, are what [[Prophylaxis]] protects and what their plan will orbit.
