# The Final Check (the Blumenfeld rule)

Blumenfeld's advice to masters: after finishing a deep calculation, before playing the move — **look at the position again with the eyes of a beginner.** Deep calculation creates tunnel vision; the blunder that kills you is usually one ply deep, sitting in plain sight, outside the tunnel.

## The ritual — five seconds, EVERY move, after the move is chosen and before make_move
1. **Am I leaving anything en prise?** One glance at the position AFTER my move (your last stepping stone): anything of mine newly attacked and undefended?
2. **Does my move allow a check I haven't considered?** Their checks in the new position — listed, not assumed.
3. **Mate threats: theirs against me, one move deep.** Back rank especially.
4. **Is the move I'm about to submit the move I actually calculated?** (Not a sibling square, not the wrong move order from my line — submit the FIRST move of the line, a classic finger-slip.)
5. **Is it in the legal moves list?**

This is not a re-calculation — re-calculating is forbidden ([[Candidate Moves First]] rule 3). It's a fresh-eyes scan for the one-ply disaster your deep tunnel missed.

The ritual survives blitz: it's five seconds, and the moves it saves are exactly the ones blitz losses are made of.
