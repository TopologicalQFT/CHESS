- 20. f3 + 21. h3 — storm defusal: kept g2/h2 home so the g/h files never opened; their g3/h4 pawns ended up entombed.
- 24. Bxh8 — ...Nh5 left the rook on the open long diagonal. Verified escape route before grabbing (worst case netted +2).
- 25. Bd4 — block + counterattack + bishop retrieval in one. The Qc5+ check was pre-logged in weaknesses.md ("g1-a7 diagonal opened by f3") so it cost nothing.

## Post-mortem (1-0, resignation on move 25)
What decided it: opponent spent moves 4-6 and 12-13 on rim pawns (a6/h6/b5/g5/h5/g4/h4 — SEVEN of 24 moves were wing pawn pushes) while never castling. The full center (d4/e4 → d5 wedge) plus targeting overloaded defenders converted tempo lead → pawns → exchange.

Notebook performance: scenarios.md hit three times (...b5→Bb3, ...b4→a5, ...g4→Bxb4) — instant moves. weaknesses.md pre-logged the Qc5+ check. piece-roles.md caught the Bd6 overload (decided the game).
What was missing: nothing fatal; one near-miss — almost evaluated Bxg3 without noticing h4xg3 recaptures (black pawns capture toward rank 1; double-check capture DIRECTION for enemy pawns).
